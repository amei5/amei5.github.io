Formula 1 Constructor Standings in the 21st Century

The FIA Formula 1 World Championship started in 1950. With a series of races held in different locations around the globe, each team (known as Constructors,) competes to finish with the most points at the end of the season. The winner is the first to cross the finish line and points are awarded based on top ten positions — the one with the most at the end of the year is crowned World Champion. In 1950 there were seven championship rounds, but as the sport has grown, there are now 24 feature 24 races in the 2024 season. We'll track the modern era of Formula 1 since 2000 and look at how constructor standings have changed over time.

There are ten teams that compete in Formula 1 – which are either privately owned or run by car manufacturers – and they must each run two cars, so 20 drivers line-up on the grid.

In the chart below, one can view the overall constructor standings for the last 23 seasons. The view can be toggled to display Position vs. Total Points as well. Hover over each bar to view more details about each constructor, the position, and points earned.

By summing up the total points earned by the drivers, the constructor with the most points at the end of the year is crowned the Constructor World Champion. To highlight the most recent 2023 season, Red Bull's Max Verstappen earned a whopping 575 points by the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix. With a combined 860 points by the end, Red Bull won the 2023 Constructor's World Championship.
